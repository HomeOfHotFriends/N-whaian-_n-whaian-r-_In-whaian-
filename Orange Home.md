It does not begin with a character, rules, enemies, goals, or even a genre.

orange field

Not in a general AI handwave way. In a very specific authored way.

The real core idea

The machine has:

1. A tiny initial logic kernel

Enough to:

recognize directionality

recognize symmetry / asymmetry

recognize timing clusters

maintain an implied position or actor-state

gradually instantiate rule-bodies


2. A small cultural grammar of game forms

Not “all games.” Just a set of authored tendencies like:

repeated single-direction input suggests motion

left-right alternation suggests axis commitment

sustained directional bias suggests traversal

directional input plus a button may suggest jump/fire/interact

analog motion suggests continuous position or aim

rhythmic taps may suggest shoot cadence or beat logic

inactivity after movement might imply inertia, waiting, or turn-readiness depending on other signals


3. Optional slot-based rule pressures

Other cartridges can bias what Henlo World is likely to become by adding rules at certain interpretive intervals.

This is huge.

Pac-Man / Galaga / CAVE host logic

You’re circling something really smart here.

You do not want a huge ontology at the start. You want a few deep inherited arcade assumptions.

Not “genre templates.” More like motion intelligences.

For example:

Pac-Man-ish logic

entities can know a player-position proxy

movement through grid or corridor commitment matters

direction changes imply intention strongly

pursuit/avoidance can exist with little information


Galaga-ish logic

a player actor can occupy a known lane/band

enemies can relate to that actor’s implied position

vertical threat and projectile logic are culturally legible


CAVE / top-plan logic

precise player location matters

bullets imply geometry of avoidance

small movement differences mean a lot

shot buttons + movement often imply a scrolling attack-space


So Henlo World could start with a handful of these arcade priors, not as full genres, but as possible interpretive skeletons.

That is very buildable, actually, compared to trying to invent infinite intelligence.
